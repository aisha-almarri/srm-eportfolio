Project Role & Responsibilities

Group Member 3: Recommendations & Summary

This unit centered on the collaborative Development Team Project: the Pampered Pets Risk Identification Report. My assigned role was Group Member 3, responsible for the final Recommendations & Summary section. This was a critical role that required synthesising the work of the entire team into a coherent, actionable conclusion for the client.

Individual Contributions

Comparative Risk Analysis

I created a concise comparative analysis table (not included in the final word count) to visually contrast the risk profiles of the status quo and the proposed digitalisation. This helped in clearly presenting the trade-offs to the business owner, Cathy.

I drew on concepts from Aven & Thekdi (2025) regarding decision-making under uncertainty to frame the recommendation. The core of the problem was not just listing risks, but evaluating whether the opportunities of digitalisation outweighed the new risks introduced.

Answering Core Business Questions

"Could an online presence grow the business by 50%?"

I argued yes, based on market reach and 24/7 availability, but qualified this by stating it was contingent on effective marketing and execution, mitigating the digital risks identified by Member 2.

"Could an international supply chain reduce costs by 24%?"

I acknowledged the potential, but heavily emphasised the new risks (delays, quality control) introduced. My recommendation was a hybrid model, leveraging international suppliers for cost while maintaining local suppliers for resilience, a concept supported by enterprise risk management principles.

"Could the business lose 33% of customers without online features?"

I supported this, citing competitive pressure and shifting consumer expectations. This directly used the "Loss of customers" risk identified by Member 1 in the status quo assessment to build a compelling case for change.

Final Recommendation and Phased Roadmap

My key deliverable was the final "go/no-go" recommendation. I recommended proceeding with a phased digitalisation, acknowledging the risks but arguing they were manageable with the proposed mitigations.

I designed the high-level implementation timeline. This demonstrated an understanding that risk management is not just about identification but also about the controlled implementation of change. The phased approach itself is a risk mitigation tactic, allowing the business to learn and adapt.

Collaboration & Teamwork

My role was inherently collaborative. I could not complete my section without the foundational work of Members 1 and 2.

Integration of Work

I actively reviewed the initial drafts from Members 1 and 2 to ensure my recommendations directly addressed their findings. For example, my emphasis on staff training in the roadmap was a direct response to the "Human Error" risk both in the current state and the digitalised future.

Peer Feedback

I provided feedback on Member 2's digitalisation risk table, suggesting that "Over-dependence on Technology" be added as a key risk, which was incorporated.

Synthesis, Not Just Summary

I ensured my section did not merely repeat the previous sections but provided a higher-level analysis. I connected Member 1's finding of "weak WiFi" to the critical need for network security as a first step in the digitalisation roadmap.

Ensuring Consistency

I worked with the team to ensure we used the NIST SP 800-30 methodology consistently throughout the report, strengthening our collective argument and the document's professional presentation.
