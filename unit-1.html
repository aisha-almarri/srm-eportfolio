Summary of Learning

This unit introduced the foundational concepts of risk. Aven & Thekdi (2025) define risk as "the effect of uncertainty on objectives," which moved me away from simply thinking of risk as 'chance of something bad happening.' I contrasted this with the more traditional, quantitative approach critiqued by Hubbard (2020), who argues that many risk management practices are fundamentally broken due to poor measurement.

Key Insights

Risk vs. Uncertainty

I now understand that risk is not just about known probabilities but also about uncertainty and the potential for surprise. This distinction fundamentally changes how we approach risk assessment and management.

Qualitative vs. Quantitative

The NIST SP 800-30 guide provides a structured, qualitative approach, while Hubbard advocates for more rigorous quantification. This tension between qualitative assessment and quantitative measurement became a key theme I followed throughout the module.

Human Element

The Hancock et al. (2024) reading on maritime rights highlighted that risk models can have real-world ethical consequences if they ignore human factors. This emphasized the importance of considering the human dimension in all risk assessments.

Application to Practice

I started a simple risk register for a personal project using a template from the NIST guide, categorising risks based on likelihood and impact. This was my first practical step in applying risk theory and demonstrated how theoretical concepts translate into practical risk management tools.
